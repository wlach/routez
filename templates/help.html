This page is meant to help users encountering some common problems.

Having trouble entering origin / destination

In order to plan a route for you, hbus needs to be able to map the location you specify into a latitude/longitude pair (this process is called geocoding).

At this time, the functionality is very much in development. We don't understand any specification other than a specific address (e.g. 5000 North Street, North & Agricola). We do not understand landmarks (e.g. Scotia Square).

The good news? This will soon be fixed. We plan to add a comprehensive set of landmarks from the OpenStreetMap dataset, as well as dynamic keyboard autocompletion of location. It's going to be awesome.

If you are entering an address that you think should work, and it doesn't, please let us know at our feedback page.

My route has too much walking!

If the route you're planning is off the main Halifax peninsula, this is almost certainly due to lack of route and stop data. Unlike transit operators such as TriMet in Portland, Oregon, or SFMTA in San Francisco, Metro Transit does not release a comprehensive set of route information to the public. To fill in the gaps, we've been manually converting the sparse schedule information posted Metro Transit's website and filling in the gaps with information gathered with a handheld GPS and a bike. The effort involved in this is great and will obviously not be completed anytime soon (though it should get better over time). If you like, you can give feedback on which routes or stops you'd like to see covered on our feedback page.

Over 20 transit providers in North America have released their route data to the public with only positive results. Wouldn't it be great if Metro Transit were to join them?
